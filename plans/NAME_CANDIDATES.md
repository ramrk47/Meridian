# Name Candidates — Brand Recommendation Doc

## 1. The brief

We are naming a **neutral, cross-platform meta-layer above India's exam-prep silos** — one place an aspirant tracks progress across every platform they pay for (Marrow, PrepLadder, Cerebellum, DocTutorials, eGurukul…), sees by consensus what's high-yield and who teaches each subject best, and is told what to do next. Shape: *"IMDB × cross-platform tracker × do-next coach for exam prep."* Launches for medical PG (NEET PG / INI-CET / FMGE) but is architected **exam-agnostic** to expand to UPSC / NEET-UG / JEE / KCET later.

Brand pillars: **neutral** ("the Switzerland above the silos"), **trustworthy**, **clarity/orientation**, **guidance/do-next**, a calm editorial **almanac** identity.

### The core trade-off: medical-flavored vs neutral-scales
- **Medical-flavored** names (Synapse, Axon, Cortex, Engram, Myelin) resonate hard with the NEET-PG launch — they feel native to doctors. But they **box in** the exam-agnostic compendium vision; "Synapse for UPSC" reads oddly.
- **Neutral / orientation / knowledge** names (Meridian, Lodestar, Almanac, Vista) **scale across every exam** and protect the long-term compendium thesis, at the cost of a slightly less visceral hook for the medical launch.

We lean **neutral-scales** (it protects the bigger vision), but include strong medical-flavored options below with an honest read — including **Synapse**, the alternative the founder floated.

### Incumbent being tested
**Meridian** is the current working name and the benchmark every candidate is measured against. It is an orientation/navigation metaphor (a meridian is a reference line of longitude — a fixed bearing, the map above the silos), scales cleanly across all exams, and is calm/editorial in tone. It is the name to **beat**, not assume away. Note: "Meridian" is itself a moderately common brand word (Meridian Health, Meridian Energy, banks) so it would need the same formal clearance as any finalist — but no NEET-PG / Indian exam-prep collision is known, and conceptually it is excellent.

## 4. The Synapse question (founder's floated alternative)

**Synapse** — the junction across which a nerve signal passes; the point of connection.

- **Why it's tempting:** visceral medical resonance (every doctor knows it), and the *connection* metaphor genuinely fits a meta-layer that connects siloed platforms. Strong, energetic, easy to say in India.
- **Honest take — two hard problems:**
  1. **Scale bucket = medical-flavored → boxes in the vision.** "Synapse for UPSC / JEE" reads wrong. It would quietly anchor the brand to medicine and undercut the exam-agnostic compendium thesis that is the company's long-term moat.
  2. **Severely crowded namespace (not formally in the vetting set, but well-known):** Synapse is one of the most reused tech/health brand words — a major (collapsed, high-profile) US fintech-as-a-service "Synapse," multiple Synapse AI/dev tools, Microsoft "Azure Synapse," and numerous health/neuro startups. The .com is long gone and SEO/app-store discoverability would be badly diluted. Expect real trademark friction in software (Class 42) and education (Class 41).
- **Verdict:** **Do not adopt as the primary brand.** If the medical launch hook is judged essential, a *coined* neuro-adjacent mark (e.g., Mnema, Cogni) carries the resonance with far less collision and scale risk. But the recommendation is to stay neutral-scales.

## 6. CAVEAT — read before any commitment

**These are directional signals from best-effort web search only. They are NOT legal or commercial clearance.** Before adopting any finalist (including Meridian and any coined mark), run all of the following:

1. **Domain availability** — live WHOIS/registrar check for `.com` and `.in` / `.co.in` / `.app` / `.co`; coined-name .coms flagged "available" above must be re-verified at decision time (availability changes daily).
2. **IP India trademark search** — ipindiaonline.gov.in, **Class 41 (education services)** and **Class 42 (software)** for text marks and close phonetic neighbors (critical given the Indian exam-prep market).
3. **USPTO trademark search** — tmsearch.uspto.gov, same classes, for international/global conflicts.
4. **App store name-availability** — Apple App Store + Google Play (and Indus Appstore) name conflicts in the Education category.
5. **Linguistic check** — no awkward/negative meaning in Hindi and major Indian languages; confirm easy pronunciation and spelling for Indian users.
6. **Trademark attorney opinion** on distinctiveness/registrability before public launch.

Treat coined names (Tovera, Wayfora, Calvetra, Lodestria, etc.) as **lowest-risk by construction**, but they still require steps 1–6.
